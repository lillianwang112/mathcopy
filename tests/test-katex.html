1. Inline Math

Einstein's famous mass-energy equivalence is $E = mc^2$, where $m$ is rest mass and $c \approx 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ is the speed of light.

The derivative of sine is $\frac{d}{dx}\sin(x) = \cos(x)$, and by the chain rule, $\frac{d}{dx}\sin(f(x)) = \cos(f(x)) \cdot f'(x)$.

Euler's identity $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$ connects five fundamental constants. The general form is $e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$.

For a right triangle with legs $a$ and $b$ and hypotenuse $c$, the Pythagorean theorem states $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

2. Display Math

The Gaussian integral evaluates to:

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2}\, dx = \sqrt{\pi}$$

The Taylor series expansion of the exponential function:

$$e^x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} = 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \cdots$$

Cauchy's integral formula from complex analysis:

$$f(a) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{\gamma} \frac{f(z)}{z - a}\, dz$$

The quadratic formula solves $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

Maxwell's equations in differential form (Gauss's law for electricity):

$$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}$$

3. Mixed Text and Math

The binomial theorem states that for any integer $n \geq 0$: $$(x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^{n-k} y^k$$ where the binomial coefficient is $\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$. Note that when $n = 2$ this gives $x^2 + 2xy + y^2$, the familiar perfect square.

Given $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ continuous on $[a, b]$ and differentiable on $(a, b)$, the mean value theorem guarantees a point $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$.

5. Fractions and Multi-line Alignment

The Fourier transform of a function $f(t)$:

$$\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)\, e^{-2\pi i \xi t}\, dt$$

Bayes' theorem: $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(B \mid A)\, P(A)}{P(B)}$. If the priors are uniform, the posterior is proportional to the likelihood.

The Schrödinger equation (time-dependent): $$i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t) = \left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2 + V(\mathbf{r}, t)\right]\Psi(\mathbf{r}, t)$$
